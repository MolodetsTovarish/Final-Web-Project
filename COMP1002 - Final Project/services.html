Uses For Fire

Cooking

Roast a delicious cut of buffalo over an open fire, create purified water by boiling, or barbeque burgers for your family gatherings. An essential part of preparing healthy, nutricious food.

Survival

Repel predators, remove pests, clear your edible supplies of bacteria. Having fire with you increases your chances of not dying from natural hazards. If you need to call for backup, use smoke signals to signal a nearby tribe or rescue team.

Heat

Insulate your body and fight against the cold with a burning flame. Sometimes a jacket just isn't enough. Huddling next to a flame is a quick solution your heat-related problems.

Light

Now nighttime is lighttime. Fire produces light, which illuminates dark, hard to see areas, allowing you to extend your daily activities for as long as you need. This is also useful for establishing shelter, such as caves.

Blacksmithing

Use fire to change the shape of metal objects to produce useful items such as swords, tools, and artwork. It changes the resources of nature to best suit your needs.

Community and Ceremony

Imagine, wrapping up the end of the day with your tribe, circling a campfire. The fire establishes an ambiance of security and an atmosphere of comraderie. It is a signal that all work for the day has ended and it is time to unwind. This prompts people to sing, tell stories, and bond when a source of fire is present.
